Module 5: Steganography

Goal: Hide existence of a message. Cover image \( I \), Stego image \( S \).

Performance Metrics

Max Payload = \( h_1 + h_2 \) (frequencies of peaks)
Embedding Capacity (bpp) = \( \frac{h_1 + h_2}{w \times h} \)
PSNR = \( 10\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{255^2}{\text{MSE}}\right) \text{ dB} \)

Prediction Error Histogram (PEH)

  • Peaks: \( P_+ \) (positive peak), \( P_- \) (negative peak).
  • Shift Non-embeddable:
    If \( r > P_+ \Rightarrow \tilde{r} = r + 1 \)
    If \( r < P_- \Rightarrow \tilde{r} = r - 1 \)
  • Embed bit \( b \):
    If \( r = P_+ \Rightarrow \tilde{r} = r + b \)
    If \( r = P_- \Rightarrow \tilde{r} = r - b \)

Prediction Formulas

Method 1 (Single): \( P_i = \sum w_j \cdot I_j \)
Method 2 (Block): Centre \( c_i \) predicts 8 satellites \( p_{i,j} \) using weighted avg of \( c_i \) and adjacent block centres.
Error: \( e_{i,j} = I_{i,j} - p_{i,j} \)

Module 5.5: Shor's Algorithm

Goal: Find factors of \( N \) in polynomial time using a quantum computer.

Order Finding (Key Idea)

Order \( r \) is the smallest positive number where:
\( x^r \equiv 1 \pmod N \)

Factorization Steps

  1. Choose random \( x \). If \( d = \gcd(x, N) \ge 2 \), return factors \( d \) and \( N/d \).
  2. Find order \( r \) such that \( x^r \equiv 1 \pmod N \).
  3. If \( r \) is even and \( d = \gcd(x^{r/2}-1, N) \ge 2 \), return factors \( d \) and \( N/d \).

Module 6: Key Establishment

Goal: Securely share a session key over a public channel.

Diffie-Hellman Protocol

Based on the Discrete Log Problem. Public params: large prime \( p \), generator \( \alpha \).

Alice: random \( a \), sends \( A = \alpha^a \bmod p \)
Bob: random \( b \), sends \( B = \alpha^b \bmod p \)
Session Key: \( k_{AB} = B^a \bmod p = A^b \bmod p = \alpha^{ab} \bmod p \)

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)

DH has no authentication. Eve intercepts \( A \) and \( B \), sends her own \( E = \alpha^e \). She shares key \( k_{AE} \) with Alice and \( k_{BE} \) with Bob.

KDC vs CA

KDC (Symmetric)CA (Asymmetric)
Shares secret key w/ usersKnows NO private keys
Generates session keysSigns public keys (certs)
Must be ONLINECan be OFFLINE
Single point of failureSafer against compromise

Module 7: Hash Functions

Goal: Map arbitrary length input \( M \) to fixed-length digest \( h = H(M) \).

3 Critical Properties

  • Pre-image Resistance (One-Way): Given \( h \), hard to find \( M \).
  • 2nd Pre-image Resistance: Given \( M_1 \), hard to find \( M_2 \neq M_1 \) where \( H(M_1) = H(M_2) \).
  • Collision Resistance: Hard to find ANY \( M_1 \neq M_2 \) where \( H(M_1) = H(M_2) \). (Implies 2nd pre-image).

Birthday Attack

Complexity to find collision: \( \approx 2^{n/2} \) attempts
SHA-256 takes \( 2^{128} \) attempts.

Algorithms & Applications

  • Broken: MD5 (128-bit), SHA-1 (160-bit).
  • Secure: SHA-2 (SHA-256/512), SHA-3.
  • Applications: Data integrity, Passwords (with salt), Digital Signatures (hash before sign), Blockchains.
  • HMAC: \( H((K \oplus \text{opad}) \| H((K \oplus \text{ipad}) \| M)) \)

Module 8: Digital Signatures

Goal: Electronic equivalent of a handwritten signature using asymmetric cryptography.

Security Services

  • Encryption Confidentiality: Kept secret.
  • Signature Integrity: Not modified.
  • Signature Authentication: Sender identity verified.
  • Signature Non-Repudiation: Cannot deny sending.

Process (Sign Hash, Not Message)

  1. Hash message: \( z = H(x) \).
  2. Sign digest with Private Key: \( y = \text{sig}_{K_{pr}}(z) \).
  3. Verify signature with Public Key: \( z' = \text{ver}_{K_{pub}}(y) \).
  4. Compare \( z' \) to \( H(x) \). Match = Valid.

RSA Signature vs DSA

  • RSA: Sign = \( x^d \bmod n \). Verify = \( y^e \bmod n \). Deterministic.
  • DSA: Based on Discrete Log. Probabilistic (uses random \( k_{E} \), signature changes every time).

RSA Homomorphic Property (Multiplicative)

RSA is partially homomorphic. Multiplying two ciphertexts and decrypting the result yields the product of their original plaintexts:

Let \( c_1 = m_1^e \bmod n \) and \( c_2 = m_2^e \bmod n \).

\( c_1 \times c_2 = (m_1^e \times m_2^e) \bmod n = (m_1 \times m_2)^e \bmod n \)

\( \text{Decryption}(c_1 \times c_2) = (c_1 \times c_2)^d \bmod n = m_1 \times m_2 \)

Module 9: ZKP, Schnorr, DKG

Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP)

Prove a statement is true without revealing the secret (e.g., Ali Baba's cave).

  • Completeness: Honest prover always convinces honest verifier.
  • Soundness: Cheating prover cannot convince honest verifier. (Prob of cheating succeeding \( n \) rounds = \( (1/2)^n \)).
  • Zero-Knowledge: Verifier learns nothing but the truth of the statement.

Schnorr Identification Protocol

Prover proves knowledge of private key \( a \) without revealing it. Setup: \( v = \alpha^{-a} \bmod p \).

1. Commit: Prover sends \( x = \alpha^k \bmod p \)
2. Challenge: Verifier sends random \( e \)
3. Response: Prover sends \( y = (k + a \cdot e) \bmod q \)
Verify: Check if \( x \equiv \alpha^y \cdot v^e \bmod p \)

Shamir's Distributed Key Generation

Split a secret \( s \) into \( n \) shares, require \( t \) shares to reconstruct.

Polynomial: \( f(x) = s + a_1x + a_2x^2 + \dots \bmod p \)
Secret is: \( f(0) = s \)
Lagrange Interpolation (at x=0):
\( s = \sum y_i L_i(0) \)   where   \( L_i(0) = \prod_{j \neq i} \frac{x_j}{x_j - x_i} \)
