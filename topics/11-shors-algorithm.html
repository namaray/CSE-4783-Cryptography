1. Introduction

Let \( N \) be a number composed of two prime numbers \( u \) and \( v \).

Shor's algorithm finds the factors of \( N \) in polynomial time. In classical computation, it takes exponential time.

🚨 Impact: RSA security is based upon a one-way function: integer factorization.

Background: Modular Arithmetic & Order of a Group

Order: Given \( N \) and \( x \), the order is the smallest positive number \( r \) such that:

\( x^r \equiv 1 \pmod N \)

Example: Let \( N = 17, x = 2 \). What is \( r \)?
\( 2^r \equiv 1 \pmod{17} \)
\( 2^8 \equiv 1 \pmod{17} \), so \( r = 8 \).

The algorithm of finding the order on a classical computer is exponential.

2. Key Idea of Shor's Algorithm

Input: \( N \)
Find: \( u, v \) such that \( N = u \times v \)

It takes a random number \( x \) and it finds the order \( r \) such that \( x^r \equiv 1 \pmod N \).

\( \Rightarrow (x^{r/2})^2 - (1)^2 \equiv 0 \pmod N \)     (if \( r \) is even)

\( \Rightarrow (x^{r/2} - 1)(x^{r/2} + 1) \equiv 0 \pmod N \)

Trivial Roots:
\( x^{r/2} = \pm 1 \)

Non-trivial Roots:
\( x^{r/2} \neq \pm 1 \)
If these square roots are non-trivial, then they will be multiples of \( u \) and \( v \). Then:

  • \( (x^{r/2} - 1) \) might be \( a \times u \)
  • \( (x^{r/2} + 1) \) might be \( b \times v \)

Therefore, we can find the factors using the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD):

\( \gcd(x^{r/2} - 1, N) = u \)
\( v = \frac{N}{u} \)

3. Shor's Algorithm (Pseudocode)

while (true) { 1. choose x ∈ {2, N-1} 2. if (d = gcd(x, N) ≥ 2) { u = d, v = N / u return u, v } 3. find r such that x^r ≡ 1 mod N 4. if (r is even && d = gcd(x^{r/2} - 1, N) ≥ 2) { 5. return u = d, v = N / u } }

4. Example Walkthrough

Let's say we want to factor \( N = 221 \).

Step 1: Choose \( x = 5 \)

Step 2: \( \gcd(5, 221) = 1 \) (Proceed to step 3)

Step 3: Find \( r \) such that \( 5^r \pmod{221} \equiv 1 \)
\( r = 16 \)

Step 4: Check if \( r \) is even (16 is even). Compute \( d \):
\( d = \gcd(5^8 - 1, 221) \)
\( d = 13 \)
So, \( u = 13 \).

Step 5: Compute \( v \):
\( v = \frac{N}{u} = \frac{221}{13} = 17 \)

Result: Factors of 221 are 13 and 17.
